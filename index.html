About me

I obtained my PhD degree from the department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at The University of Kansas in December 2019, advised by Professor Guanghui Wang. My research interests include computer vision and deep learning. On the theoretical side, I am interested in the optimization algorithms of deep learning. On the application side, my recent work is focusing on image and video understanding, including visual tracking, object detection, segmentation and 3D point cloud analysis.


I received my master from Tufts University in 2015. Before coming to US, I received my master from Graduate University of Chinese Academy of Sciences in 2012, and my B.S. in physics from Hunan Normal University in 2009.
